about me

Before I got into analytics, I spent years building actual businesses. I learned pretty quickly that spreadsheets mean nothing if they don't help you make smarter calls the next day..

I founded Dhani Agro Food Industries, ran a tofu production plant for 3 years. Managed the full production cycle and supplied 12 hotels every day, producing over 550kg per month. I figured out how to cut production costs from ₹160 to ₹100 per unit through observation, innovation and experimentation. I also got into product innovation & made kalakand (a tofu-based sweet) that originally cost ₹220/kg to make and sold for ₹350/kg. I reformulated it with gulkand and essences, which brought production cost to ₹240/kg but let us sell it at ₹400/kg. That pushed margins from 59% to 67% - an 8 percentage point jump. The daily work involved inventory planning, demand forecasting, supplier negotiations, and making sure quality stayed consistent.

At 17, I strarted ClickForAyurveda, an e-commerce website achieving 25-45% in profit margins. I sourced products, built the brand, and handled all the digital marketing - Facebook ads, Instagram content, health blogs, the whole thing. But I spent most of my time analyzing unit economics: CAC vs LTV, retention rates, demand patterns. Eventually, competition ramped up, ad costs exploded, and the math stopped making sense. I made the call to shut it down. Not easy, but it was the right move.

I worked at a co-operative credit society in Walchandnagar doing banking operations. I validated financial data across member accounts, prepared monthly statements and budget reports in Excel, and documented accounting processes. Not glamorous, but I learned how financial institutions actually work at the ground level.

I finished my BCom In Banking and Finance with an 8.83 CGPA from SPPU university. Now I'm pursuing an MCA in AI & Machine Learning at Amity University, I like solving real business problems using data, whether that's diagnosing why Spotify's algorithm keeps looping back to the same songs, or mapping out how Swiggy should expand into Tier-2 cities without bleeding cash on every order.
